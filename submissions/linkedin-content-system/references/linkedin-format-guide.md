# LinkedIn Format Guide

Use this guide to choose a suitable LinkedIn format.

## Short thought-leadership post

Best for: one insight, one tension, one practical takeaway.

Structure:

1. Specific opening observation.
2. Short development of the point.
3. Practical implication.
4. Reflective close or question.

## Promotional post

Best for: sharing a blog, event, document, model or release.

Structure:

1. Why the topic matters.
2. What the item helps with.
3. Who it is for.
4. Simple call to action if requested.

## Newsletter intro

Best for: introducing a longer article or issue.

Structure:

1. Context.
2. Tension or problem.
3. What the reader will get.
4. Smooth transition into the article.

## Long-form LinkedIn article

Best for: fuller argument, framework or experience-led piece.

Structure:

1. Clear thesis.
2. Problem and context.
3. Main argument sections.
4. Practical implications.
5. Closing reflection.

## Style safeguards

- Avoid invented authority.
- Avoid exaggerated outcomes.
- Avoid unnecessary references or links unless requested.
- Keep line breaks natural rather than formulaic.
