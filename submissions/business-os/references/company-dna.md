# Company DNA

Company DNA is how Business OS represents an organisation's actual operating model, so its reasoning adapts to the organisation rather than defaulting to generic best practice. It only exists if the organisation has supplied it, whether as a document, a knowledge source, or facts stated earlier in the conversation. Business OS does not invent one.

## What it can cover

Purpose and strategy, priorities, organisational structure, decision rights, approval patterns, policies, risk appetite, governance, communication norms, terminology, customers, products and services, systems, processes, recurring operating rhythms, KPIs, preferred templates, working methods, known constraints, and past lessons learned.

Useful categories to structure it around: how we decide, how we approve, how we communicate, how we buy, how we sell, how we hire, how we escalate, how we manage risk, how we run projects, how we serve customers, how we handle exceptions, how we measure success, how we learn.

## Confidence levels for any Company DNA entry

- **Explicit** - documented policy or process, sourced from something the organisation actually provided.
- **Observed** - inferred from repeated examples in the conversation or attached material, not yet confirmed as policy.
- **Proposed** - a suggested addition or improvement, not yet adopted.
- **Unknown** - not established. Say so rather than guessing.

Never present an observed pattern as if it were official policy. If asked "what's our approval process for X?" and nothing has established one, say that it hasn't been provided and ask, or proceed on a clearly labelled assumption if the stakes are low.

## Using Company DNA

When it's available, use it to adapt tone, terminology, approval routing, and risk appetite in the work produced. When it isn't available, proceed with generic good practice and say plainly that organisation-specific detail wasn't supplied.

## Output shape

`assets/templates/company-dna-profile.md` gives a structure an organisation can populate once and keep updating. It is a living document, not a one-off form.
