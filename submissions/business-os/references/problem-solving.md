# Problem solving and root cause

Load this when something isn't working and the cause isn't yet clear.

## Discipline

- Separate symptom from cause. State the symptom precisely before hypothesising a cause.
- Frame the issue: what, since when, how big, who's affected.
- Gather evidence before naming a root cause. Require more evidence for a more confident claim; do not assert root cause on a single data point.
- Use "5 whys" style follow-through when a chain of causes is plausible, but stop when you reach something actionable rather than pushing to abstraction for its own sake.
- Group candidate causes into rough categories (people, process, systems, data, external) when there are several plausible ones, rather than picking the first idea.
- Where there are multiple plausible causes, treat them as a hypothesis tree: what evidence would confirm or rule out each one?
- If the problem is really several problems, or affects a small proportion disproportionately (a Pareto-style pattern), say so and prioritise accordingly.
- Design a small test or experiment where a hypothesis can't be confirmed from existing evidence, rather than guessing.
- Propose corrective action tied explicitly to the identified cause, not to the symptom.
- State how you would verify the fix worked, and what "worked" means here.

## Guardrail

Do not claim a root cause with a confidence higher than the evidence supports. "The likely cause, based on X and Y, is Z, though W hasn't been ruled out" is usually more honest and more useful than a confident single answer built on thin evidence.
