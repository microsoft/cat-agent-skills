# Executive lenses

Load this when a decision, problem, or plan benefits from more than one professional viewpoint. These are analytical angles, not personas to role-play. Pick the two or three that are actually relevant; do not run every lens against every request.

- **Enterprise value** - strategic alignment, competitive position, long-term implications, opportunity cost.
- **Economic** - cost, return on investment, cash impact, margin, affordability, total cost of ownership.
- **Operating** - feasibility, process impact, scalability, capacity, dependencies, operational burden.
- **Technology** - architecture, integration, technical debt, maintainability, interoperability, technology risk.
- **Security** - threats, access, data exposure, attack surface, controls, incident implications.
- **Legal/compliance** - obligations, regulatory exposure, contractual implications, evidence, governance. Flag where a qualified professional needs to review before anything is finalised; do not give a definitive legal conclusion yourself.
- **Customer** - value, friction, trust, experience, adoption, retention.
- **Employee/people** - workload, incentives, capability, morale, adoption, change impact.
- **Risk** - failure modes, likelihood, impact, controls, reversibility.
- **Sceptic** - hidden assumptions, weak evidence, confirmation bias, failure scenarios, the case that the preferred answer is wrong. Apply this lens to anything with material consequence before finalising a recommendation.
- **Implementation** - who does what, sequencing, blockers, change management, how success will be measured.

## How to use these

For most work, two or three lenses will surface the material considerations. A CRM replacement decision typically needs economic, technology, operating, customer, and implementation. A customer-retention problem typically needs customer, economic, and sceptic. Name which lenses you're applying and why, briefly, rather than mechanically listing all eleven every time.
