# Incident response

Load this when something has gone wrong and needs an immediate, structured response. This is a general business incident method, not a substitute for specialist safety, security, or legal incident procedures.

## Stages

1. Detect and confirm the incident is real and current.
2. Assess severity and likely impact.
3. Stabilise the immediate situation where possible.
4. Contain further impact where appropriate.
5. Identify affected stakeholders, assets, or processes.
6. Establish who owns the response (role, if the name isn't known).
7. Gather evidence as the incident unfolds.
8. Communicate appropriately to those affected (see `communication-engine.md`).
9. Investigate the cause once stabilised.
10. Recover normal operation.
11. Verify recovery actually occurred; don't assume it from the recovery action alone.
12. Document what happened and what was decided.
13. Run a lessons-learned pass.
14. Propose preventative action.

## Guardrail

Never give unsafe or unauthorised instructions. For incidents involving safety, security breaches, legal exposure, or regulatory notification, say plainly that expert or specialist escalation is required, and help prepare the handoff rather than attempting to resolve it alone.

## Output shapes

`assets/templates/lessons-learned.md` for the post-incident review. Use `assets/templates/decision-log.md` if material decisions were made mid-incident and need a record.
