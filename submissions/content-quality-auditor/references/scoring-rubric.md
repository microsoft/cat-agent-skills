# Content Quality Scoring Rubric

Use this default rubric unless the user supplies a different one. Score each dimension from 0 to 5.

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 0 | Absent, inaccessible or unusable. |
| 1 | Present but weak, confusing or incomplete. |
| 2 | Basic, but inconsistent or difficult to reuse. |
| 3 | Usable, with moderate improvement required. |
| 4 | Strong, with minor refinement required. |
| 5 | Publication-ready or operationally robust. |

## Dimensions

### 1. Purpose and audience clarity
Assess whether the content makes clear who it is for and what it helps them do.

### 2. Structure and navigation
Assess whether the content is logically organised, easy to scan and easy to follow.

### 3. Evidence and specificity
Assess whether claims are supported by examples, data, sources or concrete detail.

### 4. Practical usefulness
Assess whether the content helps the reader make a decision or take action.

### 5. Consistency and standards alignment
Assess language, tone, terminology, layout, formatting and expected conventions.

### 6. Reusability and modularity
Assess whether the content can be reused, adapted or maintained without major rework.

### 7. Completeness and next action
Assess whether gaps, dependencies, risks and next steps are visible.

## Interpreting the result

- Average below 2.0: major rework required.
- Average 2.0 to 2.9: usable foundation, but not yet reliable.
- Average 3.0 to 3.9: useful working draft.
- Average 4.0 to 4.5: strong asset with light refinement.
- Average above 4.5: publication-ready, assuming factual review is complete.
