# Follow-up Sequencer Playbook

Plan and write a **multi-touch outreach cadence** across email and social DMs for one prospect, from first contact to a clean stop. Use this after (or together with) the cold-email or social-DM playbook when the user wants more than a single message.

## Default cadence (adapt to the user's constraints)

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email | Cold email (via cold-email playbook) |
| 2 | Day 3–4 | Email | Thoughtful bump: short, references touch 1, no pressure |
| 3 | Day 7–9 | Social DM | Connection/first message with a different hook than the email |
| 4 | Day 14 | Email | New-value touch: share one genuinely useful, sourced insight for their situation, no ask |
| 5 | Day 21–25 | Email or DM | Polite breakup: close the loop respectfully, leave the door open |

Rules for adapting: max 5–6 touches per sequence; at least 3 days between touches on the same channel; every touch must add something new (new hook, new insight, new proof point) — never "just checking in"; switch channels at most twice.

## Workflow

1. Confirm inputs: prospect briefing (or ask for the essentials), sender's company profile, channels available, any timing constraints (holidays, campaign windows, legal quiet periods).
2. Produce the cadence plan as a table: touch number, day, channel, goal, hook.
3. Write the full text for every touch, applying the matching playbook of this suite (cold-email rules for emails, social-DM rules for DMs). Global hard rules apply to every touch.
4. Define the **stop conditions** explicitly: any reply pauses the sequence immediately; an objection routes to the objection-handling playbook; an unsubscribe-type signal ("please stop contacting me") ends all outreach to that person permanently.
5. If the prospect replies positively, draft the next step: thank briefly, propose a low-friction conversation with 2–3 concrete time windows with generous lead time, keep it under 60 words.

## Hard rules

- Respect the target market's outreach norms and applicable rules (e.g. consent requirements for email in some jurisdictions); if the user's setup looks non-compliant, flag it once instead of silently proceeding.
- The breakup touch is mandatory in every full sequence: sequences that fade out without closure or continue indefinitely are not allowed.
- Never escalate pressure across touches; persistence comes from new value, not from urgency theater.
- Output is a plan plus drafts for the user to schedule and send; this playbook never sends or schedules anything itself.
