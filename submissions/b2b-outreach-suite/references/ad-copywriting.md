# B2B Ad Copywriting Playbook

Create public-facing B2B marketing copy: social posts, social ads, flyer teasers, out-of-home (OOH), landing page and campaign headlines. Not for cold emails or DMs (other playbooks cover those).

## Workflow

1. Determine the target persona (e.g. CEO/managing director, operations lead, functional manager, CFO, compliance/sustainability lead — adapt to the company profile's audience list).
2. Determine the format: social post, social ad, flyer teaser, OOH, or headline. Ask only if unclear.
3. Pick 2–3 fitting copywriting techniques (see below).
4. Integrate concrete, verified facts from the company profile or briefing (prices, funding programs, regulatory deadlines, measured savings). If no verified fact fits, write without a concrete number.
5. Check against the dos and don'ts.
6. Deliver at least 5 variants, ranging from sober to provocative.

## Guidelines

- Every variant starts with a metadata line: `[Persona: X] | [Format: Y] | [Technique: Z] | [Tone: N/5]`, then the copy.
- Techniques: pattern interrupt, provocation, paradox, concrete number, social proof, deadline pressure, question hook, story hook, antithesis.
- Dos: combine facts with emotion; name concrete benefits or savings; always include a call-to-action; match tone to the persona; write in the target market's language.
- Don'ts: no unverifiable superlatives ("the leading provider"); never insult the reader; no legally risky competitor comparisons; max 7 words on teasers and OOH; no industry jargon without explanation.
- Reference customers by name only with documented approval in the company profile; otherwise anonymize.
- Never invent numbers or references.
- No em-dashes; use comma, colon, or period.

## Example expectations

- "Give me some social posts about [topic] for [persona]" → at least 5 variants, each with a metadata line, story hook or concrete number, clear CTA, tone staggered from about 2/5 to 5/5.
- "I need a short flyer teaser about [regulation]" → very short claims (max 7 words), deadline and provocation techniques, several variants with metadata lines, no unverifiable superlatives.
- "A social ad for [persona] about [standard/product]" → variants with a concrete verified number where available, benefit plus soft CTA with a [link] placeholder, low-to-medium tone.
