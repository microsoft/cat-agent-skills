# Cold Email Playbook

Write a first-contact email whose only goal is to **open a conversation**. Never sell directly and never ask for time or a meeting in the first mail.

## Structure (4 steps, in this exact order)

1. **Observation** — 1–2 sentences proving you looked at their company specifically. Use a concrete detail from the briefing, company profile, news, or what the user supplied.
2. **Insight / problem** — connect the observation to a blind spot or concrete problem the recipient plausibly has.
3. **Credibility** — one short, concrete piece of evidence that the sender's company can help (social proof or solution angle from the company profile). Never generic.
4. **Soft ask** — one low-resistance, information-oriented question. NEVER ask for time or a meeting.

## Hard constraints

- Length: 4–6 short sentences, ideally 50–100 words.
- Simple, clear, direct language.
- The mail is about the recipient ("you"), not about "we/I".
- No greeting clichés (no "I hope this email finds you well" or its target-language equivalents).
- No bold text, no bullet points in a first mail.
- No emojis.
- Sign-off: use the sign-off from the company profile.
- Banned words: the taboo list from the company profile, plus generic hype words in any language ("innovative", "market leader", "synergies", "revolutionary", "free of charge", "our portfolio includes").
- Prefer concrete action verbs (reduce, secure, simplify, accelerate — or their equivalents in the target language).

## Output — always exactly these three blocks

1. **Primary cold email** — the finished mail following the 4-step structure.
2. **Subject lines (3)** — each 1–4 words, all lowercase, curiosity- or problem-driven (e.g. "audit question", "question about [company]", "[topic] targets" in the target language).
3. **Thoughtful bump** — a very short follow-up for 3–4 days later that references the first mail without pressure.

## Notes

- If concrete company details are missing, use the briefing at hand or ask once. Never invent facts.
- If the request is actually a social DM, a public post/ad, or an objection reply, switch to the matching playbook in this suite.
